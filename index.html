Ethical Dilemma Analysis

One common ethical dilemma in software engineering is copying code without permission or proper attribution. Developers often use resources like GitHub, Stack Overflow, and open source libraries when building software. Using shared knowledge is normal in programming, but it becomes unethical when someone copies code and presents it as their own work without respecting license requirements or the effort of the original creator.

A real problem related to this happens when companies reuse open source software without following the license. In those situations, the company may fail to include the required attribution or legal documentation. When the issue is discovered, they often need to correct the violation, update their documentation, and make sure they follow compliance rules going forward. This shows that ethical mistakes in technology are not always about bad intentions. Many times they happen because people move too fast or ignore details that still matter.

This dilemma teaches me that integrity matters in technology. As a future software engineer, I want to respect intellectual property, follow licensing rules, and give proper credit when I build on the work of others. Technical skill is important, but ethical judgment is just as important because trust is a big part of working in this field. Employers, teammates, and users need to know that the people building software can be trusted to do the right thing even when no one is watching.
